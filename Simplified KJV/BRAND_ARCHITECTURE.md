- **Viral Product:** Based King James Version (BKJV) -- the physical Bible, the meme, the Trojan horse

- Sees a TikTok of @BasedBible explaining `dunamis` in a 15-second clip
- Laughs. Shares. Comments "I need this Bible."

### Phase 3: Enrollment (supernatural.institute)
- Lands on supernatural.institute. Free tier: "Power Word Foundations."
- Completes 6-week course on healing theology and covenant power.

### Phase 4: Integration (supernaturalchurches.org)
- Searches "supernatural church near me" on supernaturalchurches.org
- Finds Australian Supernatural Churches directory
- Attends a local affiliated church or joins online community
- Eventually plants a church under regional oversight

## Brand Separation Rules (CRITICAL)

### What BKJV Does
- Sells Bibles
- Posts memes
- Creates viral content
- Uses internet slang and cultural references
- Has fun, irreverent, attention-grabbing marketing

### What BKJV Never Does
- Never mentions supernaturalchurches.org directly in marketing
- Never solicits donations for "the ministry"
- Never presents itself as a church or denomination
- Never uses pastoral authority language
- The product IS the ministry's front door, not the ministry itself

### What supernaturalchurches.org Does
- Provides pastoral oversight
- Credentials ministers
- Plants churches
- Hosts conferences
- Maintains doctrinal standards
- Handles finances, governance, legal compliance

### What supernaturalchurches.org Never Does
- Never references BKJV in formal communications
- Never sells Bibles directly (students may receive them, but the org doesn't sell)
- Never uses meme language, slang, or ironic tone
- The ministry IS the destination, not the marketing

### What supernatural.institute Does
- Educates believers
- Certifies ministers
- Bridges the gap between "Bible buyer" and "church member"
- Can reference BKJV as "the recommended text" for courses
- Can reference supernaturalchurches.org as "our governing body"

## The Risk You Must Accept

Running two brands means twice the work, twice the social media management, twice the legal exposure, and the constant risk of crossover (someone screenshots a BKJV post on a supernaturalchurches.org thread).

**Mitigation:** Different people run each brand. BKJV is a product company with a marketing team. supernaturalchurches.org is a ministry with pastoral staff. supernatural.institute is an education company with instructors. If you're one person, this is overwhelming. If you have a team, it's manageable.

**If you are one person:** Start with BKJV only. Build the product. Sell it. Create the content. Let the organic traffic to supernaturalchurches.org happen without a formal institute. Add the institute and regional bodies after BKJV is cash-flow positive.
