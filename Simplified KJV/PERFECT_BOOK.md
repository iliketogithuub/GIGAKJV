# ⚡ QUICK START GUIDE
Need Healing TODAY? Start Here.

If you're facing a health crisis right now, don't wait to read the whole book. Start here, and let the Holy Spirit begin the work immediately while you build your foundation.

BEFORE YOU START: FORGIVE (MARK 11:25–26)

Jesus connected mountain-moving faith with forgiveness. Before you pray, say this out loud:

"Father, in Jesus' name, I forgive every person I've held anything against. I release them now. I refuse to hold unforgiveness, bitterness, or offence. Thank You that as I forgive, You hear my prayer of faith."

Now you are ready to stand in faith without blockage in your heart.

STEP 1: DECLARE THIS NOW (Say It Out Loud)

"Jesus, I believe You bore my sickness at the whipping post. By Your stripes, I am healed—past tense, finished, done. I don't need to beg; I receive what You already provided. I am healed in Jesus' name. Amen."

STEP 2: STOP SAYING

Stop agreeing with sickness and fear. Refuse to let these sentences come out of your mouth:

"I'm sick."
"I'm getting worse."
"The doctor said this is the end."
"I hope I get better one day."
"This always happens to me."

STEP 3: START SAYING (Every Hour if Needed)

Instead, put God's Word in your mouth. Say things like:

"By His stripes, I am healed."
"Jesus already bore this sickness so I don't have to."
"I walk in divine health; my body is the temple of the Holy Spirit."
"God's Word is my final report, not my symptoms or doctor's report."
"No weapon formed against me shall prosper, in Jesus' name."

STEP 4: TAKE COMMUNION TODAY

Get bread and juice (or water).

Hold the bread and say:
"This is Jesus' body broken at the whipping post for my healing. I receive everything His stripes purchased for me. By His stripes I am healed."
Then eat it.

Hold the cup and say:
"This is Jesus' blood shed for my forgiveness and my covenant with God. I receive full forgiveness, full righteousness, and full access to everything Jesus paid for."
Then drink it.

STEP 5: STAND FIRM

Symptoms may scream. Fear may attack your mind. People may contradict your faith. Do not move off the Word. Your healing is not something you're trying to get; it is something Jesus already provided. You are standing until what He finished shows up in your body.

STEP 6: GUARD YOUR WORDS

Faith is released by words and actions. If you speak faith for healing and then say, "I'm still sick," you are undermining the very thing you believed for. Don't cancel your confession with unbelief.

I want you to picture your mouth like a gun and your words like bullets. Every time you speak, something is being fired. Some bullets carry life. Some bullets carry death. There are no neutral bullets.

Proverbs 18:21 says death and life are in the power of the tongue. That means every sentence you fire lands in one of two categories: life or death. Even if it sounds only "a little bit negative," it still goes in the death category. It either agrees with God's Word or it agrees with the problem.

When you say things like, "I have cancer," "My diabetes," or "This sickness is killing me," you are firing death bullets into your own body. Say it enough times, and those words start to feel permanent. You load your heart and mind with bullets that agree with the curse instead of the cross.

On top of that, other people often start shooting bullets at you too:

"How's the cancer going?"
"You know this runs in your family."
"Most people never get better from that."

If you keep agreeing with those statements, it's like standing still while others fire at you and then loading more bullets into yourself with your own mouth. How many bullets can a person take before it destroys their expectation of divine health?

Instead, load your mouth with life bullets. Let your words fire the truth of God's Word, not the lies of symptoms:

"By His stripes I was healed."
"I have no sickness in Jesus' name."
"I walk in divine health; the life of God flows in my body."

The more you speak life, the more your heart is convinced of what God already said, and the more your body lines up with that truth.

People talk about addiction to sugar, caffeine, or entertainment, but the most dangerous addiction is an addiction to speaking negative. James says the tongue is like a bit in a horse’s mouth and a rudder on a ship—it steers everything. With the same tongue people bless God and then speak curses over themselves and others. "My back is killing me." "This always happens to me." James said this should not be so.

You are not called to have an unbridled tongue. You are called to bridle your tongue with the Word of God. To **bridle** your tongue simply means to put a bit and reins on it—to control what it says and where it goes, the same way a rider controls a horse. An **unbridled** tongue is a mouth with no control, like a wild horse running wherever it wants, saying whatever it feels.

Break the addiction to speaking death. Build a holy addiction to speaking life—God’s Word only. Refuse to let your mouth feed on negative talk like a sugar craving. Starve it. And feed on Scripture until speaking life becomes your new normal.

If you catch yourself speaking fear or doubt, don't condemn yourself—repent and realign:

"Lord, I cancel those words in Jesus' name. I choose to agree with Your Word: by Your stripes I am healed."

Then go back to speaking life. Refuse to fire another death bullet with your mouth. Stay on the side of faith until your body lines up with what Jesus already finished.

Now, as you continue through this book, you'll learn how to build an unshakeable foundation in divine healing and divine health so this becomes your lifestyle, not just a crisis response.

## PART ONE: WHAT'S MISSING

# CHAPTER ONE
## The Gospel You've Been Taught
*(It's Not Wrong, Just Incomplete)*

What you learned about salvation is true. I'm not taking that away. I'm adding what was left out.
The gospel you heard—Jesus' blood washing away sins, giving eternal life—is real and biblical.
It's just not the whole story.
 So before we talk about what’s missing, let’s make sure we’re crystal clear on what you already have.
 The Blood Gospel
Here’s what most churches preach, and rightly so:
1. YOU WERE A SINNER.
Romans 3:23 (NKJV): "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."
Every person born into this world (except Jesus) has sinned. We’ve all done wrong. We’ve all fallen short. We were all guilty before a holy God.
But if you’ve accepted Christ? That’s your OLD identity.
2 Corinthians 5:17 (NKJV): "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new."
You are not a sinner anymore. You are a son or daughter of God (1 John 3:1). Your spirit was born again. You are a new creature. You are an alien on this earth, a citizen of heaven (Philippians 3:20).
Your old sin nature is dead and gone. The challenge now is learning to live from your new identity as a child of God. Let's walk in that truth together.
2. THE PENALTY FOR SIN IS DEATH.
Romans 6:23 (NKJV): "For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."
Sin isn’t just a mistake. It’s a debt. And the payment is death—not just physical death, but eternal separation from God.
3. BUT GOD LOVED YOU SO MUCH, HE SENT JESUS.
John 3:16 (NKJV): "For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life."
This is the most famous verse in the Bible for a reason. It’s the heart of the gospel. God didn’t want you to perish, so He sent Jesus to pay the price you couldn’t pay.
4. JESUS DIED IN YOUR PLACE.
Romans 5:8 (NKJV): "But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."
Jesus didn’t wait for you to clean up your act. He died while you were still a sinner. He took your punishment. He bore your sin. He shed His blood so you wouldn’t have to face death.
5. IF YOU BELIEVE AND CONFESS, YOU ARE SAVED.
Romans 10:9-10 (NKJV): "That if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved. For with the heart one believes unto righteousness, and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation."
Salvation is simple. Believe in your heart. Confess with your mouth. You’re saved. Born again. Made new. A child of God.
This is the gospel most churches preach. And it’s true.
What the Blood Purchased
Let’s be very clear about what Jesus’ blood accomplished for you:
Forgiveness of All Sin
Ephesians 1:7 (NKJV): "In Him we have redemption through His blood, the forgiveness of sins, according to the riches of His grace."
Every sin you’ve ever committed—past, present, future—is forgiven. Wiped clean. Removed as far as the east is from the west.
Justification Before God
Romans 5:9 (NKJV): "Much more then, having now been justified by His blood, we shall be saved from wrath through Him."
You’re not just forgiven. You’re justified. That means in God’s eyes, it’s just-as-if-you’d-never-sinned. You stand before God as if you’ve never done anything wrong.
Redemption from the Curse
Galatians 3:13 (NKJV): "Christ has redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us (for it is written, 'Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree')."
Jesus became a curse so you could be free from the curse. He took what you deserved so you could receive what He deserved.
Eternal Life
1 John 5:11-12 (NKJV): "And this is the testimony: that God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has life; he who does not have the Son of God does not have life."
You’re not just saved from hell. You’re given eternal life—life that starts now and never ends.
Peace with God
Colossians 1:20 (NKJV): "And by Him to reconcile all things to Himself, by Him, whether things on earth or things in heaven, having made peace through the blood of His cross."
The war is over. You’re no longer God’s enemy. You have peace with Him through the blood of Jesus.
This Is Good News
If all you ever heard was what I just told you, it would be enough to shout about. The blood of Jesus is powerful. It cleanses. It justifies. It redeems. It saves.
But here’s my question: Is that all Jesus died for?
I’m going to put it another way: If all Jesus wanted to do was forgive your sin and give you eternal life... Why did He have to suffer so much BEFORE He went to the cross?
The Part We Skip Over
Most sermons go straight from the Last Supper to the crucifixion. We talk about Jesus in the garden praying. We mention Judas betraying Him. Then we jump to the cross.
But something happened in between that most preachers rush past.
 Let me show you what Jesus endured before He was crucified:
 HE WAS BEATEN.
 Matthew 27:26 (NKJV): "Then he released Barabbas to them; and when he had scourged Jesus, he delivered Him to be crucified."
 The word "scourged" sounds almost peaceful. But it was brutal.
 Scourging was brutal. They tied Jesus to a post and whipped Him with a leather strap covered in bone, metal, and glass pieces. Each lash ripped His flesh. They gave Him 39 lashes. Many men died from this alone. Jesus took it for you.
 Jesus endured this.
HIS BEARD WAS RIPPED OUT.
Isaiah 50:6 (NKJV): "I gave My back to those who struck Me, and My cheeks to those who plucked out the beard; I did not hide My face from shame and spitting."
This wasn’t shaving. This was violent pulling. Tearing hair out by the roots. Adding insult to injury. Humiliation on top of the physical torment.
HE WAS MOCKED AND STRUCK.
Matthew 27:28-30 (NKJV): "And they stripped Him and put a scarlet robe on Him. When they had twisted a crown of thorns, they put it on His head, and a reed in His right hand. And they bowed the knee before Him and mocked Him, saying, 'Hail, King of the Jews!' Then they spat on Him, and took the reed and struck Him on the head."
The crown of thorns wasn’t just placed on His head. It was beaten into His skull. Every strike drove those thorns deeper. Blood poured down His face.
HE CARRIED HIS CROSS.
John 19:17 (NKJV): "And He, bearing His cross, went out to a place called the Place of a Skull, which is called in Hebrew, Golgotha."
After being scourged, beaten, and mocked, Jesus carried a wooden cross beam (weighing 75-125 pounds) through the streets of Jerusalem. His shredded back rubbed against the rough wood with every step. He collapsed under the weight. They had to force someone else to carry it for Him.
Why All the Suffering?
Here’s the question most people never ask: Why did Jesus have to go through all of that?
If the only issue was sin, couldn’t Jesus have just gone straight to the cross? Wouldn’t His blood alone be enough?
Here’s the answer: The blood dealt with your sin. The stripes dealt with your sickness.
I want to prove it.
The Prophecy
700 years before Jesus was born, the prophet Isaiah wrote this:
Isaiah 53:4-5 (NKJV): "Surely He has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows; Yet we esteemed Him stricken, Smitten by God, and afflicted. But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities; The chastisement for our peace was upon Him, And by His stripes we are healed."
Look at the structure of this passage:
"Wounded for our transgressions" = dealing with sin
"Bruised for our iniquities" = dealing with sin
"Chastisement for our peace" = dealing with sin
"By His stripes we are healed" = dealing with... what?
Not sin. Sickness.
The Hebrew word for "griefs" is choliy (Strong’s H2483)—it means sickness, disease, malady.
The Hebrew word for "sorrows" is makob (Strong’s H4341)—it refers to physical suffering.
Isaiah 53:4 in its literal Hebrew meaning: "Surely He has borne our sicknesses and carried our physical torments."
This isn’t poetry. This isn’t symbolic. This is a prophecy about what Jesus would physically endure and what it would accomplish.
The Fulfillment
Fast forward 700 years. Jesus is walking the earth. He’s healing people everywhere He goes. Blind eyes open. Deaf ears hear. Lame people walk. Lepers are cleansed.
Why?
Matthew 8:16-17 (NKJV): "When evening had come, they brought to Him many who were demon-possessed. And He cast out the spirits with a word, and healed all who were sick, that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by Isaiah the prophet, saying: 'He Himself took our infirmities And bore our sicknesses.'"
Did you catch that? Matthew, inspired by the Holy Spirit, quotes Isaiah 53:4 and applies it to Jesus healing the sick. Not forgiving sin. Healing sickness.
Jesus bore your sicknesses. He carried your diseases. He took them on Himself so you wouldn’t have to carry them.
Two Provisions, Not One
Let me make this as simple as I can. Imagine a doctor’s bill with two charges:
Surgery to remove a disease = $50,000
Medicine to prevent it from coming back = $50,000
Total bill: $100,000
Now imagine someone walks in and says, "I’ll pay the whole thing." They write a check for $100,000. The bill is paid in full. You’re free to go.
But when you leave, the receptionist only gives you the surgery receipt. She keeps the medicine receipt in the drawer. You go home thinking, "Wow, I got the surgery for free! But I guess I still have to pay for the medicine myself."
That’s what the church has been doing. Jesus paid for BOTH.
The blood paid for your sin (eternal life).
The stripes paid for your sickness (divine health).
But most Christians only know about the first one.
What You’ve Been Given
Let me be very clear: When you got saved, you didn’t just receive forgiveness. You received a package deal.
> **1 Peter 2:24 (NKJV): "Who Himself bore our sins in His own body on the tree, that we, having died to sins, might live for righteousness—by whose stripes you were healed."**
Notice:
"Bore our sins" = past tense, done deal
"You were healed" = past tense, done deal
Both were purchased at the same time. Both are finished. Both are yours.
The Question
So here’s what I want you to ask yourself: If Jesus paid for both my sin and my sickness... Why am I only walking in one? Why am I forgiven but not walking in divine health? Why do I have eternal life but no health in this life?
Here’s the simple truth: Nobody told you the detailed truth of the other half of the gospel.
Even though you’ve heard "by His stripes you were healed," it may have been difficult to reconcile with past experiences. You’ve seen others suffer. You’ve probably seen people in wheelchairs rolling up to church and rolling back out again, hoping for a miracle.
They CAN get it if they take hold of this truth and receive it themselves—without anyone’s help.
And that’s what we’re about to fix.
 What’s Next
 In the next chapter, we’re going to dive deep into what the church left out. We’re going to see exactly how the stripes of Jesus purchased your healing. We’re going to prove it from Scripture after Scripture after Scripture.
 And by the time we’re done, you’re going to realize: You already own what you’ve been praying for. You just didn’t know it was yours.
 **NOW SAY THIS OUT LOUD:**
 Before you go any further, say this out loud:
"Jesus didn’t just die for my sin. He also died for my sickness. At the whipping post, His body was broken for my healing. At the cross, His blood was shed for my forgiveness. Both provisions are mine. I don’t have to choose one or the other. I receive BOTH. By His stripes, I was healed—past tense, finished work. By His blood, I am forgiven—past tense, finished work. Healing isn’t something I’m begging for. Healing is something I already have. I just didn’t know it until now. But now I know. And knowing changes everything. In Jesus’ name. Amen."
Good. You just agreed with 700 years of prophecy and 2,000 years of fulfillment. That’s a solid foundation.
As you move forward with this truth in your heart, continually declare the dual provision of Jesus: Your healing through His stripes and your salvation through His blood. This is the foundation that will sustain you.
Now speak out loud: I'm healed by the stripes of Jesus. I'm saved by the blood of Jesus.
NOTE: If you’ve never given your life to Jesus:
You can skip to the final chapter right now. Read the salvation prayer. Say it from your heart. Make Jesus Lord. Then come back and keep reading.
God doesn’t force Himself on anyone. But if you want what He has to offer... healing, forgiveness, eternal life... you have to receive Him. That’s the deal. Your choice.

# CHAPTER TWO
## The Half They Left Out

I’m going to tell you what the enemy has been doing.
He knows he can’t take your salvation. Once you’re born again, you’re sealed by the Holy Spirit. You’re a child of God forever.
But he tries to hinder your walk in divine health.
Not because he has the right to. Not because God gave it to him. But because you don’t know it’s yours.
The Bible says:
Hosea 4:6 (NKJV): "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge."
The devil doesn’t have to fight you if you don’t even know what belongs to you.
It’s like this: Imagine you inherited a million dollars, but nobody told you. The money is sitting in a bank account with your name on it. But you’re living paycheck to paycheck, struggling to pay bills, stressed about money. You’re broke while you’re rich, simply because you don’t know.
That’s what’s happening with healing. You’ve been redeemed from sickness. It’s been paid for. It’s yours. But if you don’t know it, you won’t walk in it.
And the devil is perfectly happy to keep you from walking in divine health and strength—not because he has the power to keep you from it, but because you don’t know you have the power to be free.
Two Gifts, One Package
Let me make this as simple as I possibly can. When you got saved, you received a package deal.
GIFT ONE: Eternal Life (through the blood)
Forgiveness of sin
Justification before God
Peace with God
Right standing with God
Eternal life in heaven
GIFT TWO: Divine Health (through the stripes)
Manifestation of healing
Complete freedom
Divine health and strength
Restoration of your body
Authority over the devil’s oppression
You don’t get one without the other. You can’t say, "I’ll take eternal life, but I’ll skip the healing." It’s a package. It was all purchased at once. It’s all yours.
Romans 8:32 (NKJV): "He who did not spare His own Son, but delivered Him up for us all, how shall He not with Him also freely give us all things?"
If God gave you Jesus, He gave you everything Jesus purchased. You don’t have to pick and choose. You already have it all.
What You Need to Do
Here’s what I want you to understand: You don’t have to pray for God to heal you. He already did. You don’t have to beg for it. You don’t have to convince Him. You don’t have to earn it.
It’s been done. It’s finished. It’s yours.
What you need to do is receive it.
The same way you received salvation. You didn’t earn salvation. You didn’t work for it. You didn’t beg God to forgive you. You believed what Jesus did, and you received it by faith.
Healing works the same way. Jesus already bore your sickness. The price is paid. The work is done. Now you just need to believe it and receive it.
The Question You’re Asking
I know what you’re thinking: "If it’s that simple, why isn’t everyone walking in divine health?" "Why am I forgiven but not walking in divine health?"
Great questions. And we’re going to answer them in the chapters ahead.
But first, I need you to settle this in your heart: Healing is in the atonement. It’s been purchased. It’s been provided. It’s part of the gospel.
Once you know that—once you really believe that—everything changes. You stop begging God to do something He’s already done. You stop doubting whether it’s His will (it is). You stop wondering if you’re good enough (you’re not, but Jesus is). And you start walking in what’s already yours.
But What About...?
I know some of you’re already thinking of objections. "What about Paul’s thorn in the flesh?" "What about Job?" "Doesn’t God use sickness to teach us?"
I want to dismantle these religious sacred cows right now, because if I don’t, the devil will use them to hinder your understanding of what belongs to you.
OBJECTION ONE: "Paul’s Thorn in the Flesh"
People love to point to 2 Corinthians 12:7-10 and say, "See? Even Paul experienced a physical challenge that God wouldn’t remove."
Let’s read it:
2 Corinthians 12:7-9 (NKJV): "And lest I should be exalted above measure by the abundance of the revelations, a thorn in the flesh was given to me, a messenger of Satan to buffet me, lest I be exalted above measure. Concerning this thing I pleaded with the Lord three times that it might depart from me. And He said to me, 'My grace is sufficient for you, for My strength is made perfect in weakness.'"
Here’s what people miss:
Paul calls it "a messenger of Satan" - not a gift from God
The Greek word for "messenger" is angelos (Strong’s G32) - it means angel, messenger, or agent. This wasn’t a physical ailment. It was a demonic agent sent to harass Paul.
The word "buffet" means to strike with the fist repeatedly
The Greek word kolaphizo (Strong’s G2852) means to strike with the fist, to beat, to mistreat. This is violent opposition, not sickness.
Look at what Paul actually suffered
2 Corinthians 11:23-27 (NKJV): "...in labors more abundant, in stripes above measure, in prisons more frequently, in deaths often. From the Jews five times I received forty stripes minus one. Three times I was beaten with rods; once I was stoned; three times I was shipwrecked; a night and a day I have been in the deep; in journeys often, in perils of waters, in perils of robbers, in perils of my own countrymen, in perils of the Gentiles, in perils in the city, in perils in the wilderness, in perils in the sea, in perils among false brethren; in weariness and toil, in sleeplessness often, in hunger and thirst, in fastings often, in cold and nakedness."
That’s the thorn. Everywhere Paul went to preach the gospel, Satan sent opposition: Beatings, Stonings, Shipwrecks, False brethren, Persecution.
The thorn wasn’t sickness. It was persecution.
And when Paul asked God to remove it, God said: "My grace is sufficient. My strength is made perfect in weakness."
Translation: "You’re going to face opposition, but My grace will get you through it."
Paul’s thorn has nothing to do with physical ailments. Stop using it as an excuse to not walk in divine health.
OBJECTION TWO: "What About Job?"
"Job was righteous, and he experienced physical affliction. So, some might conclude that physical affliction must be acceptable."
Three problems with this argument:
Job lived BEFORE the cross
Job didn’t have the blood of Jesus. He didn’t have the New Covenant. He didn’t have the redemption we have. You can’t use Old Testament examples to override New Testament promises.
God didn’t cause Job’s affliction - Satan did
Job 2:7 (NKJV): "So Satan went out from the presence of the Lord, and struck Job with painful boils from the sole of his foot to the crown of his head."
Satan struck Job. Not God.
And notice: God restored Job completely and gave him double everything he lost.
Job 42:10, 12 (NKJV): "And the Lord restored Job’s losses when he prayed for his friends. Indeed the Lord gave Job twice as much as he had before... Now the Lord blessed the latter days of Job more than his beginning."
God’s will was restoration, not affliction.
Job himself declared that affliction wasn’t from God
Job 33:23-25 (NKJV): "If there’s a messenger for him, a mediator, one among a thousand, to show man His uprightness, then He is gracious to him, and says, 'Deliver him from going down to the Pit; I have found a ransom'; his flesh shall be young like a child’s, he shall return to the days of his youth."
Even in the midst of suffering, Job prophesied about a coming ransom (Jesus) who would restore flesh and youth. Job looked forward to what we have now. Don’t use Job’s suffering to reject what Jesus purchased for you.
OBJECTION THREE: "God Uses Sickness to Teach Us"
This is the most blasphemous lie in the church. Let me be very clear: God doesn’t use physical ailments to teach you patience. God doesn’t use severe physical conditions to draw you closer to Him. God doesn’t allow children to suffer physical afflictions to teach their parents lessons. That’s demonic doctrine.
Here’s why:
Acts 10:38 (NKJV): "How God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and with power, who went about doing good and healing all who were oppressed by the devil, for God was with Him."
Physical oppression is a work of the devil.
Jesus spent His entire ministry destroying the works of the devil.
1 John 3:8 (NKJV): "For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that He might destroy the works of the devil."
If physical affliction is from God, then Jesus was working against His Father by bringing healing to people. That’s absurd.
James 1:17 (NKJV): "Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and comes down from the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shadow of turning."

### Now Let’s Break It Down

We are going to focus on verses 4–6 because that is where the dual provision becomes crystal clear.

#### Verse 4: The Foundation

Isaiah 53:4 (NKJV): "Surely He has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows; yet we esteemed Him stricken, smitten by God, and afflicted."

Let’s look at the key words:

- **Surely** — Hebrew *aken* (H403) = truly, certainly, absolutely. Isaiah is shouting: “Pay attention. This is a fact.”
- **He has borne** — Hebrew *nasa* (H5375) = to lift, bear, carry, take away, suffer. The very same verb is used in verse 12: "And He bore the sin of many." What He did with your sin, He also did with your sickness.
- **Our griefs** — Hebrew *choliy* (H2483) = sickness, disease, physical malady. Not emotional grief. Literal sickness. See Deuteronomy 7:15; Deuteronomy 28:61; 2 Chronicles 21:15.
- **And carried** — Hebrew *sabal* (H5445) = to carry as a burden. The same word is used in verse 11 for "bearing iniquities." Again, sin and sickness are treated the same way.
- **Our sorrows** — Hebrew *makob* (H4341) = pain, physical suffering. Job 33:19 uses the exact word for physical pain in the bones.

**Literal rendering:** “Surely He has borne our sicknesses and carried our pains.” This is not symbolic. Jesus literally lifted physical sickness off you and carried it Himself.

#### The New Testament Confirms It

Matthew 8:16–17 (NKJV) quotes Isaiah 53:4: "He Himself took our infirmities and bore our sicknesses." The Holy Spirit applied that verse to Jesus **healing the sick**, not forgiving sin. There is zero debate about what Isaiah meant.

#### Verse 5: The Dual Provision

Isaiah 53:5 (NKJV): "But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities; the chastisement for our peace was upon Him, and by His stripes we are healed."

Four clauses. Four provisions:

1. **He was wounded for our transgressions** — *chalal* = pierced through (nails, spear). Deals with sin.
2. **He was bruised for our iniquities** — *daka* = crushed to powder. Deals with sin.
3. **The chastisement for our peace was upon Him** — *musar* for *shalom* (wholeness, nothing missing). Deals with our total restoration.
4. **By His stripes we are healed** — *chaburah* (wounds, lashes) producing *rapha* (healing, curing, repairing). Deals with physical healing.

The pattern could not be clearer:

- Wounded for our transgressions = BLOOD / SIN
- Bruised for our iniquities = BLOOD / SIN
- Chastisement for our peace = BLOOD / RESTORATION
- By His stripes we are healed = BODY / HEALING

> **Atonement** literally means "at-one-ment." Jesus’ sacrifice reconciled you to God by dealing with **both** sin and sickness in the same transaction.

#### The Word “Healed” (*Rapha*)

Every time *rapha* appears it describes physical healing (Exodus 15:26, 2 Kings 20:5, Psalm 103:3, Jeremiah 17:14, Malachi 4:2). “By His stripes we are healed [rapha]” means visible, tangible healing in your body.

Peter agrees: **1 Peter 2:24** — "By whose stripes **you were** healed." Past tense. The Greek word *iaomai* also speaks of physical cures (Matthew 8:8, Luke 9:11, Acts 10:38). Your healing was purchased at the whipping post the same moment forgiveness was purchased at the cross.

#### Verse 6: Why It Had to Happen

Isaiah 53:6 (NKJV): "All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned, every one, to his own way; and the Lord has laid on Him the iniquity of us all."

- All of us sinned.
- All of us went our own way.
- The Father deliberately **laid** (Hebrew *paga*, H6293) our iniquity on Jesus.

This is the exchange. Jesus takes everything you deserved, and you take everything He deserved.

### The Big Picture

- Verse 4: Jesus bore sickness and pain.
- Verse 5: Jesus was wounded for sin and whipped for healing.
- Verse 6: The Father placed the whole world’s iniquity on Him.

Four witnesses (Isaiah 53, Matthew 8:17, 1 Peter 2:24, Psalm 103:3) establish this in court. Healing is just as legally yours as forgiveness.

**EXEGETICAL ERROR #1: "Perfect" Doesn't Mean "Completed Bible"**

Greek: teleion (τέλειον - Strong's G5046) = complete, mature, perfect, lacking nothing

"Then" = when we see Jesus FACE TO FACE.
Not: when we have a completed book.

Paul is contrasting:
- NOW = partial knowledge, seeing dimly
- THEN = complete knowledge, seeing face to face

No honest Greek scholar can twist "face to face" to mean "having a Bible."

**EXEGETICAL ERROR #2: The Canon Argument Is Historically False**

Historical fact: The New Testament canon wasn't officially recognized until the Council of Carthage in AD 397.

Question for cessationists: Were Christians between AD 100-397 living without "that which is perfect"?

If "perfect" = completed Bible, then Christians had NO "perfect" for 300 years.

But historical evidence shows: Miracles continued throughout those 300 years.

**EXEGETICAL ERROR #3: Jesus Never Set an Expiration Date**

Show me where Jesus said:
- "These signs will follow believers... for about 70 years."

He didn't. Because that's NOT what He meant.

"Those who believe" = NO TIME LIMIT.
No expiration clause.
No "first century only" disclaimer.

**EXEGETICAL ERROR #4: The "Authenticating Signs" Argument Fails**

Cessationists claim: "Miracles authenticated the apostles. Once authenticated, they're unnecessary."

Problem: That's not what Scripture says miracles are FOR.

John 14:12 (NKJV): "Most assuredly, I say to you, he who believes in Me, the works that I do he will do also; and greater works than these he will do, because I go to My Father."

Jesus said: "He who believes in Me WILL DO the works that I do."

That's philosophy, not theology.

### OBJECTION #8: "Choliy and Makob Could Be Metaphorical"

**THE CLAIM:** "The Hebrew words in Isaiah 53:4 could mean 'emotional grief' or 'spiritual sickness,' not physical healing."

**THE SCRIPTURE:**

Hebrew:

**THE LINGUISTIC DEMOLITION:**

**CHOLIY (H2483) - Every Other Usage in Scripture**

This word appears 24 times in the Old Testament. In EVERY OTHER INSTANCE, it refers to PHYSICAL sickness.

Deuteronomy 7:15: "The Lord will take away from you all sickness [choliy]"
Context: Physical diseases of Egypt (boils, tumors, plagues)

Deuteronomy 28:61: "Also every sickness [choliy] and every plague"
Context: Physical consequences of disobedience

1 Kings 17:17: "The son of the woman became sick [choliy]"
Context: Physical illness leading to death

2 Kings 1:2: "Will I recover from this sickness [choliy]?"
Context: King Ahaziah's physical injury from falling

2 Kings 8:8-9: "Shall I recover from this sickness [choliy]?"
Context: King of Syria's physical illness

2 Chronicles 16:12: "Asa was diseased [choliy] in his feet"
Context: Physical foot disease

2 Chronicles 21:15: "You will become very sick [choliy] with a disease of your intestines"
Context: Intestinal disease

Isaiah 38:9: "When he had been sick [choliy] and had recovered"
Context: King Hezekiah's physical illness

Jeremiah 6:7: "Before Me continually is grief [choliy] and wounds"
Context: Physical violence and injury

Jeremiah 10:19: "Woe is me for my hurt! My wound [choliy] is severe"
Context: Physical wound/injury

**CONCLUSION:** Choliy ALWAYS means physical illness. Zero textual justification for "emotional grief" or "spiritual sickness."

**MAKOB (H4341) - Every Other Usage in Scripture**

This word appears 16 times in the Old Testament. ALWAYS refers to physical pain or suffering.

Job 33:19: "Man is also chastened with pain [makob] on his bed, and with strong pain in many of his bones"
Context: Physical pain in bones

Psalm 38:17: "And my sorrow [makob] is continually before me"
Context: Psalmist's physical suffering from illness (verses 3-7)

Psalm 69:26: "They talk of the grief [makob] of those You have wounded"
Context: Physical wounds

Jeremiah 30:15: "Why do you cry about your affliction [makob]? Your sorrow is incurable"
Context: Physical affliction

Jeremiah 45:3: "The Lord has added grief [makob] to my sorrow"
Context: Physical distress and anguish

Jeremiah 51:8: "Take balm for her pain [makob]; perhaps she may be healed"
Context: Physical injury requiring medical treatment

**CONCLUSION:** Makob ALWAYS refers to physical pain or suffering. No exception.

**THE SEPTUAGINT CONFIRMS IT**

The Septuagint (Greek Old Testament, translated 250 BC) translates:
- Choliy as nosos (νόσος) = sickness, disease (used 11 times in NT for physical illness)
- Makob as ponos (πόνος) = pain, suffering (always physical)

The ancient translators understood Isaiah 53:4 as PHYSICAL sickness.

**MATTHEW 8:17 SETTLES IT**

Matthew 8:16-17 (NKJV): "When evening had come, they brought to Him many who were demon-possessed. And He cast out the spirits with a word, and healed all who were sick, that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by Isaiah the prophet, saying: 'He Himself took our infirmities and bore our sicknesses.'"

Matthew, inspired by the Holy Spirit, quotes Isaiah 53:4 and applies it to Jesus healing PHYSICAL sickness.

Greek words used:
- Astheneia (ἀσθένεια - G769) = physical weakness/sickness
- Nosos (νόσος - G3554) = physical disease

Both ALWAYS refer to physical illness in the New Testament.

**ACADEMIC FOOTNOTE:**

For scholars: The Masoretic text, Dead Sea Scrolls, and Septuagint all AGREE on the wording of Isaiah 53:4. There is ZERO textual variance that would support a "spiritual sickness" interpretation.

This is not debatable among honest scholars.

**CONCLUSION:** Isaiah 53:4 refers to PHYSICAL sickness. The Hebrew, Greek, and New Testament interpretation all confirm it. Anyone claiming it's "metaphorical" is eisegeting (reading into) rather than exegeting (reading out of) the text.
