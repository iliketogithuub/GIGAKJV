These images are screenshots from the official Simplified KJV website (simplifiedkjv.com) showing side-by-side comparisons of the King James Version (KJV) and the Simplified King James Version (SKJV). They appear to be promotional "look inside" images demonstrating the readability improvements.

## Image Descriptions

### Image Set 1: `simplified-kjv-website-look-inside-comparison_orig.png` & `(1)`

These two images appear to show a single two-page spread comparison. The layout shows:

- **Left page:** KJV text with traditional formatting
- **Right page:** SKJV text with modernized formatting

The comparison demonstrates:
- Removal of archaic verb endings (-eth, -est)
- Modern punctuation (periods replacing colons/semicolons)
- Updated pronouns (thee/thou/ye → you)
- Paragraph breaks added for readability
- Quotation marks around spoken dialogue
- Divine pronouns capitalized

The specific passage shown appears to be from the Old Testament, likely from Genesis or Exodus, demonstrating the contrast between the dense, continuous-block KJV text and the more accessible, paragraphed SKJV text.

### Image Set 2: `simplified-kjv-website-look-inside-comparison-2222_orig.png` & `(1)` & `(2)`

These three images appear to show another set of comparison pages, possibly from the New Testament or a different Old Testament passage. The "2222" in the filename may refer to a specific page number or image ID from the website's content management system.

The visual comparison again emphasizes:
- Shorter sentences in the SKJV
- Modern verb forms
- Clearer paragraph structure
- Updated vocabulary where archaic terms would confuse modern readers
- Retention of the KJV's underlying translation decisions

## General Observations

All five images serve the same purpose: marketing the SKJV by visually demonstrating that the text remains faithful to the KJV while being significantly easier to read. The page designs show:

1. **KJV side:** Dense text, minimal paragraphing, archaic punctuation, old verb forms
2. **SKJV side:** Modern paragraphing, shorter sentences, contemporary vocabulary, quotation marks for dialogue
