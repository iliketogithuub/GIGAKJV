## 1. THE SUPERNATURAL GOSPEL: Forgiveness AND Healing
*Adapted from "THE STRIPES" by Samuel Waterhouse*

### The Gospel You Were Taught (It’s Not Wrong, Just Incomplete)
What you learned about salvation is true. No one is taking that away. We are adding what was left out. The gospel you heard—Jesus’ blood washing away sins, giving eternal life—is real, biblical, and absolute. It is just not the whole story.

Before we talk about what is missing, let’s be crystal clear on what you already have in **the Blood Gospel**:
1. **You were a sinner.** *Romans 3:23* states: "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." Every person born into this world (except Jesus) has sinned. We were all guilty before a holy God. But if you have accepted Christ, that is your *old* identity. *2 Corinthians 5:17* declares: "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, that individual is a new creation; old things have passed away; look, all things have become new." You are not a sinner anymore. You are a child of God (*1 John 3:1*). Your spirit was born again.
2. **The penalty for sin is death.** *Romans 6:23*: "For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." Sin is a legal debt, and the payment is death—not just physical death, but eternal separation from God.
3. **God sent Jesus to pay the debt.** *John 3:16*: "For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life."
4. **Jesus died in your place.** *Romans 5:8*: "But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us." Jesus took your punishment. He shed His blood so you would not have to face death.
5. **Belief and confession activate salvation.** *Romans 10:9-10* states that if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved.

This is the gospel most churches preach. It is powerful, and it purchased:
- **Forgiveness of all sin** (*Ephesians 1:7*)
- **Justification before God** (*Romans 5:9* — "just-as-if-I'd-never-sinned")
- **Redemption from the curse** (*Galatians 3:13*)
- **Eternal life** (*1 John 5:11-12*)
- **Peace with God** (*Colossians 1:20*)

If this were all Jesus died for, it would be enough to praise Him for eternity. But here is the question most believers never ask: **Why did Jesus have to suffer so brutally BEFORE He went to the cross?**

### The Part We Skip Over
Most sermons jump straight from the Garden of Gethsemane to the crucifixion. They bypass the physical scourging, treating it as a historical footnote. But look at what Jesus endured before the nails pierced His hands:
- **He was scourged.** *Matthew 27:26*: "...when he had scourged Jesus, he delivered Him to be crucified." Scourging was not just a whipping. Roman scourging tied a victim to a post and ripped their back to shreds with a whip containing glass, metal, and bone. Many men died from this alone.
- **His beard was ripped out.** *Isaiah 50:6*: "I gave My back to those who struck Me, and My cheeks to those who plucked out the beard..."
- **He was mocked and struck on the head.** *Matthew 27:28-30*. A crown of long, sharp thorns was beaten into His skull.

If the only issue was sin, couldn't Jesus have gone straight to the cross? Why the scourging? Why the whipped back?

Here is the answer: **The blood dealt with your sin. The stripes dealt with your sickness.**

### The Prophecy and the Fulfillment
Seven hundred years before Jesus was born, the prophet Isaiah outlined the dual nature of the atonement:
> "Surely He has borne our **griefs** and carried our **sorrows**; yet we esteemed Him stricken, smitten by God, and afflicted. But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities; the chastisement for our peace was upon Him, and **by His stripes we are healed**."
> — *Isaiah 53:4-5*

The original Hebrew words reveal the exact nature of this contract:
- The word for "griefs" is **choliy** (*Strong's H2483*), which literally means physical sickness, disease, and malady.
- The word for "sorrows" is **makob** (*Strong's H4341*), which literally means physical pain.
- Isaiah 53:4 literally reads: *"Surely He has borne our sicknesses and carried our physical pains."*

Matthew, under the inspiration of the Holy Spirit, confirms this translation:
> "When evening had come, they brought to Him many who were demon-possessed. And He cast out the spirits with a word, and healed all who were sick, **that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by Isaiah the prophet, saying: 'He Himself took our infirmities and bore our sicknesses.'**"
> — *Matthew 8:16-17*

Matthew does not apply Isaiah 53:4 to the forgiveness of sin. He applies it directly to the physical healing of the body. Jesus did not just take your sin on the cross; He took your sickness at the whipping post. 

### Two Provisions, One Sacrifice
Imagine a doctor's bill with two charges: a surgery charge of $50,000 and a medication charge of $50,000. If someone pays the entire $100,000, both are settled. But if you only walk out with the surgery receipt and keep paying for the medication out of pocket, you are living below the provision that was already bought for you.

This is the state of the modern church. Believers are forgiven (sin paid for) but are begging and pleading for physical healing, unaware that the receipt for their health was signed at the Roman whipping post.

*1 Peter 2:24* connects both in the past tense:
> "Who Himself bore our sins in His own body on the tree, that we, having died to sins, might live for righteousness—**by whose stripes you were healed**."

Notice the syntax:
1. "Bore our sins" — past tense, finished work.
2. "You were healed" — past tense, finished work.

You are not waiting for God to decide whether He wants to heal you. He already made that decision 2,000 years ago when He laid your diseases on Jesus' back. Your healing is not a future event you are trying to make happen; it is a past-tense legal reality you must now enforce.

## 2. HOW TO TAKE COMMUNION FOR PHYSICAL HEALING
*A Practical Covenant Protocol*

Communion is not a religious ritual; it is a legal enforcement of the New Covenant. It is a physical action that releases spiritual power. If you need healing in your body today, do not take communion passively. Take it with covenant authority.

### Preparation: Cleanse Your Heart
Jesus connected mountain-moving faith with the absence of bitterness. Before you take the elements, say this aloud to clear any legal blockages:
> *"Father, in the name of Jesus, I choose to forgive every person who has wronged me, mistreated me, or spoken evil against me. I release them from all debt. I refuse to hold a grudge, bitterness, or offense. As I forgive, I stand before You with a clean heart, ready to receive the covenant provisions of my Lord. Amen."*

### Step 1: The Bread (The Body for Healing)
Take a piece of bread or a cracker. Hold it in your hands. 
Look at the bread and declare:
> *"Lord Jesus, I hold in my hand the representation of Your body. Your body was scourged, torn, and broken at the whipping post for me. You took my cancer, my pain, my disease, and my weakness into Your own flesh. Because You bore it, I do not have to carry it. By Your stripes, I was healed. I eat this bread now and receive the physical infusion of Your life, strength, and health into my flesh. In Jesus' name, amen."*
*(Eat the bread)*

### Step 2: The Cup (The Blood for Righteousness)
Take the cup (juice, wine, or water). Hold it in your hands.
Look at the cup and declare:
> *"Lord Jesus, I hold in my hand the cup of the New Covenant in Your blood. Your blood was shed to wash away my sins, to justify me before the Father, and to place me in perfect right-standing with God. Because of Your blood, I have legal access to the throne of grace. I drink this cup now and receive my righteousness, my forgiveness, and my absolute authority over the works of darkness. Sickness has no legal right to remain in my body, because my body is the temple of the Holy Spirit. In Jesus' name, amen."*
*(Drink the cup)*

### Step 3: The Confession of Life
Do not cancel your communion by speaking sickness and defeat later in the day. Keep your mouth aligned with the finished work. Fire "life bullets" from your mouth:
- *Refuse to say:* "My cancer," "My arthritis," "I am getting worse," "I guess it's just my luck."
- *Say instead:* "By His stripes I was healed. Sickness, you have no legal right to my body. I command you to pack up and leave in the name of Jesus. I walk in divine health!"

## 3. HOW TO USE THIS TRANSLATION
The **Based King James Version (BKJV)** is engineered for action. It strips away religious tradition and archaic vocabulary to expose the functional power of the text. To read it correctly, you must understand its design features:

### A. The Zero-Pronoun Rule (Instruction Blocks)
To eliminate theological smoke screens and emphasize personal application, third-person pronouns (`he`, `she`, `they`, `their`, `his`, `him`) in direct instruction and covenant promise blocks are systematically replaced with concrete, active nouns:
- *"his heart"* $\rightarrow$ *"their heart"* or *"the heart"*
- *"he shall have"* $\rightarrow$ *"that individual will have"* or *"the believer will have"*
- *"them that believe"* $\rightarrow$ *"the believers"*

This prevents you from reading the Bible as a story about *someone else* and forces you to read it as a universal law addressed directly to *you*.

### B. The Power Word Distinction
The Greek text of the New Testament uses distinct words for power that traditional translations lump together:
1. **Exousia** (Delegated Authority / Legal Standing): Translated in the BKJV as **delegated authority** or **legal authority**. It represents your badge of office. You do not beg for strength; you enforce your legal authority.
2. **Dunamis** (Inherent Supernatural Force / Miraculous Ability): Translated in the BKJV as **supernatural power** or **miraculous power**. It represents the explosive muscle of the Holy Spirit.

### C. Bold Functional Directives
Whenever a verse outlines an explicit command, action step, or state of identity under the Covenant, the core action words are **bolded**.
- *Example:* "For truly I tell you, whoever **says to this mountain**, 'Be lifted up and thrown into the sea,' and **does not doubt within their heart**..." (*Mark 11:23*)

This shows you exactly what your spirit must execute.

### D. Bracketed Operational Insights
After key passages outlining a spiritual mechanism, you will find a brief, italicized `[Operational Insight]` explaining the legal and spiritual mechanics of how that verse operates in real life. These are designed to bypass theological debate and give you the raw operational protocol.
