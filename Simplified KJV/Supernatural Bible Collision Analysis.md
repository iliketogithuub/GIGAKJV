## What It Actually Is

The Passio "Supernatural Bible" is NOT a new translation. It is a **study Bible** built on top of the **Modern English Version (MEV)** -- a formal equivalence translation from the Textus Receptus produced in 2014 by Charisma House itself. The MEV is essentially Charisma's answer to the NKJV.

### Contents
- 95 pages of front matter on the supernatural, Israel, and spiritual disciplines
- Original Hebrew names: Yeshua (Jesus), Miriam (Mary), shofar (trumpet)
- Chain-referenced commentaries by **Dr. Sandra Kennedy** and **Dr. Michael Brown** on healing and Messianic prophecies
- Commentaries by **Sid Roth** on baptism in the Holy Spirit
- Commentaries by **Dr. Keith Ellis** on the Glory
- Sid Roth's favorite Scriptures for meditation
- Sid Roth's articles on personal mentoring and motivation
- Red letter for Jesus' words
- 12-point font
- 8.5" x 5.5" x 2" thick

### Key Point
This is a **branded study edition of the MEV**, not a distinct translation. Passio/Charisma owns the MEV, so they created a "Supernatural" branded package around it using their own in-house personalities (Sandra Kennedy, Sid Roth, Michael Brown).

## My Honest Assessment

**The Passio "Supernatural Bible" discovery is a near-fatal blow to the "Supernatural Bible" brand.** Not because you can't legally use the words (you can -- "supernatural" and "Bible" are generic), but because **Charisma Media owns the mindshare**. Any charismatic Christian who searches "supernatural Bible" will find Sid Roth, Sandra Kennedy, and Michael Brown. They will not find you.

**The irony:** Charisma's "Supernatural Bible" is a study Bible -- commentaries wrapped around the MEV. Your project is a **translation**. You're doing harder, more original work. But the market doesn't reward harder work. It rewards brand recognition.

**The decision comes down to this:**

- If you want **media attention and controversy**, you need a name that **doesn't already belong to a bigger player**. "Supernatural Bible" fails this test worse than BKJV.
- If you want **ministry alignment**, you need "supernatural" in the name, but you can't use "Bible" without colliding with Passio.
- If you want **to ship quickly**, keep BKJV and accept the Barbour collision as the smaller of two evils.

**My recommendation:**

1. **Abandon "Supernatural Bible" and "TSB" immediately.** Charisma won this battle in 2017.
2. **Abandon "SuperKJV"** if you were heading toward "The Supernatural King James Version" -- "Supernatural Bible" searches will still drown you out.
3. **Choose one of two paths:**
   - **Path A:** Keep "Simple King James Version" but change the acronym to **SiKJV** or **SimpKJV**. Fight Barbour for the KJV space. This is the "ship fast" path.
   - **Path B:** Go fully independent with **"The Covenant Power Bible" (TCPB)** or **"The Supernatural Churches Bible" (TSCB)**. No KJV. No "Bible" collision. Build a new category. This is the "go big" path.

**Path B is the only one that generates the media attention you want.**

A brand new Bible translation called "The Covenant Power Bible" with a front-matter gospel of divine health and a power word lexicon is **unprecedented**. Nobody has done this. It will force every charismatic reviewer to engage with it because there's no existing brand to compare it to.

"The Supernatural Bible" will be dismissed as "oh, that's like Passio's thing but cheaper."  
"The Covenant Power Bible" will be met with "wait, what is that?"

The second question is the one that sells books.
